Departments and Facilities

Cardiology

Cardiology

Hospitals that specialise in the diagnosis, treatment, and management of heart-related illnesses and conditions have departments dedicated to cardiology. Cardiologists are medical professionals with specialised knowledge of the cardiovascular system who work in departments dedicated to this field. Their duties encompass the assessment, identification, and management of patients with cardiac ailments.

Anesthesiology

Anesthesiology

Anesthesiology is a branch of medicine that treats patients undergoing surgery or other medical procedures by administering anaesthesia and providing perioperative care. A patient's heart rate, blood pressure, breathing, and other physiological functions are all under the control of anesthesiologists during a surgical procedure. Anaesthesia is given to ensure that patients are pain-free and comfortable during the procedure.

ENT Specialists

ENT Specialists

Ear, Nose, and Throat, or ENT, is the abbreviation for the otolaryngology medical specialty. The diagnosis and treatment of disorders pertaining to the head, neck, and ears is the primary focus of ENT specialists, also known as otolaryngologists. They deal with problems like voice disorders, sinus problems, hearing loss, and other conditions that affect the head and neck area.

Neurotrauma

Neurotrauma

The term "neurotrauma" describes wounds to the nervous system, specifically to the brain and spinal cord. There are several reasons why these injuries may occur, such as trauma, falls, accidents, or acts of violence. Serious outcomes from neurotrauma include spinal cord injury (SCI) and traumatic brain injury (TBI). In order to help people recover function and adjust to life after neurotrauma, rehabilitation is essential.

Urology

Urology

Urology is a branch of medicine that specialises in the diagnosis and treatment of diseases affecting the male reproductive system, the urinary tract in both genders, and both. Urologists are qualified to treat a broad variety of conditions, such as kidney stones, bladder troubles, prostate problems, UTIs, and issues related to men's reproductive health. Urology includes specialties like paediatric urology, female urology, and urologic oncology. They may employ surgical or medical procedures.

Oncosurgery

Oncosurgery

Surgical oncology, also known as oncosurgery, is a subspecialty of surgery that treats cancer. Surgical oncologists are doctors who specialise in performing surgical procedures to remove cancerous tissues or tumours from the body. Oncosurgery aims to remove cancerous cells from the body, stop them from spreading to other areas, and, if it's feasible, treat or cure the patient's cancer. To provide comprehensive cancer care, surgical oncologists frequently collaborate with other cancer specialists, including medical oncologists and radiation oncologists.

General & Laparoscopic Surgery

General & Laparoscopic Surgery

Surgical procedures on different body parts are included in the broad category of general surgery. Small incisions and specialised instruments are used in laparoscopic surgery, a minimally invasive procedure that has advantages over open surgery such as less pain and faster recovery. The needs of the patient often dictate which procedures general surgeons perform, whether they are traditional or laparoscopic.

Rheumatology

Rheumatology

The medical specialty of rheumatology is dedicated to the diagnosis and treatment of illnesses that impact the muscles, connective tissues, and joints. Rheumatologists are medical professionals with a focus on treating autoimmune diseases like lupus, arthritis, and others. To treat rheumatic disease patients' symptoms, lower inflammation, and enhance their overall quality of life, a range of strategies are used, such as medication, physical therapy, and lifestyle modifications.

Physiotherapy

Physiotherapy

The goal of the medical field of physical therapy, or physiotherapy, is to maximise function and movement. Physical therapists, also known as physiotherapists, assess and treat a range of illnesses, wounds, and disabilities with their patients. Physiotherapy seeks to increase mobility, reduce pain, and improve general physical well-being through exercises, manual therapy, and other methods. In order to promote and maintain health and fitness, it is essential and is frequently used in rehabilitation following surgery, injury, or illness.

Orthopedics & Arthroscopy

Orthopedics & Arthroscopy

The medical specialty of orthopaedics treats disorders of the musculoskeletal system, which includes the bones and joints.
Arthroscopy: Quicker recovery through the use of a tiny camera during minimally invasive surgery to identify and treat joint problems. Comparing arthroscopy to open surgery, the former usually yields shorter recovery periods and less pain following surgery.

Critical Care

Critical Care

Patients with severe and potentially fatal illnesses or injuries require comprehensive care. This is known as critical care. The intensive care unit (ICU), also known as the critical care unit, is where this specialist medical care is offered. In addition to providing close monitoring and support for vital organ systems, critical care teams, which comprise critical care physicians and nurses, also handle urgent and complicated medical needs. Stabilise patients, stop further decline, and aid in their recovery are the objectives.

Gastro-enterology

Gastro-enterology

The medical specialty that focuses on disorders of the digestive system is called gastroenterology. Conditions affecting the gastrointestinal tract, which includes the oesophagus, stomach, intestines, liver, gallbladder, and pancreas, are diagnosed and treated by gastroenterologists. Gastroenterologists frequently treat conditions like acid reflux, liver illnesses, gastrointestinal malignancies, and inflammatory bowel illnesses (like Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis).

Plastic & Reconstructive Surgery

Plastic & Reconstructive Surgery

The restoration, reconstruction, or modification of the human body is the focus of the medical specialty known as plastic and reconstructive surgery. Plastic surgeons treat a variety of diseases, such as birth defects, severe trauma, and cosmetic issues. The field includes both aesthetic procedures (i.e., to improve appearance) and reconstructive procedures (i.e., to restore function and appearance following illness or injury).

Obstetrics & Gynecology

Obstetrics & Gynecology

The field of medicine known as obstetrics and gynaecology, or OB/GYN, is dedicated to the care of the female reproductive system. In order to ensure the health of mother and child, obstetricians specialise in the care of pregnant women, childbirth, and postpartum care. Gynaecologists specialise in treating disorders of the non-pregnant female reproductive system, including endometriosis and cervical cancer, as well as family planning and the health of reproductive organs.

Neurology

Neurology

Disorders of the nervous system, which comprises the brain, spinal cord, nerves, and muscles, are treated by the medical specialty known as neurology. A wide range of illnesses, including headaches, multiple sclerosis, epilepsy, stroke, and Alzheimer's disease, are diagnosed and treated by neurologists. In order to provide complete care, they frequently collaborate closely with other medical specialists and employ a variety of diagnostic techniques, such as imaging studies and neurological exams.
